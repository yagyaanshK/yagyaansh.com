Welcome back everyone!!
In this blog I’ll be focusing mainly on the basic needs of a person – “Food, Clothes and Shelter” ( उर्फ़ रोटी, कपड़ा, और मकान ). Before I begin, I want to mention that (probably) I am the most inexperienced person to be writing this blog because I have lived in this place (IITR) only for 2 months ,i.e., only (1/24)th of the time that I am going to spend here. But the whole purpose of these series of blogs is to enable the world to peep in and know a little about the life of a fresher at IITR in his/her initial days.


Hostel Rooms ‐ This is probably the first thing that you might be curious to know about. Boys will be alloted rooms in Rajendra Bhawan based on their JEE Ranks. Most of them are going to get twin sharing rooms, and some of them will get single rooms. Girls will be alloted twin sharing rooms in the Sarojini Bhawan.

[UPDATE 2021: The pattern for allotment of single rooms for boys might change for each incoming batch. But from 2nd year onwards, all boys have a single room. Hostel allocation depends on your branch and campus politics. Girls are alloted rooms in the Kasturba Bhawan from 2nd year onwards. As new hostels get constructed, this pattern might also change.]



The building of our Bhawan (Hostel) is more than 80 years old. After seeing the building for the first time, all hopes of a good hostel fade away instantly. But wait, the worst is yet to come. You open the door of your room and boom! You get to see the typical infrastructure that you expect from a “government” building in India. Everything is wearied out. The study table, the bed & the almirah (it doesn’t even qualify to be called an almirah) looked like they had been abused for a millennium (I apologize if I go too far). To worsen the situation further, my room is adjacent to the bathroom which makes my room even more vulnerable to termite (which, the supervisor mentioned, is a problem in the whole campus!). There are more lizards in the corridors than there are students living there (And no doubt, the lizards look more well-fed than the students living there). Though the bathrooms are cleaned every day, their condition is pathetic. Have a look ‐



Yep, get ready for Indian toilets. Indeed, they are the only hygienic option when so many people are sharing the bathrooms.

Food ‐ The next important thing which bothers all of us is food. The mess food in Rajendra Bhawan (all first yearite boys are given this hostel) is decent enough. However, there might be opposing views from some other students. This is how the mess infrastructure looks like ‐



I can’t comment much on the taste as we all might have very different opinions for the same food. But for those who think that mess food sucks, it doesn’t. In fact, every week you get to eat paneer, ice-cream, kheer, gulab juman, etc. though they are served as if diamond and gold are being distributed to us. South Indian food is rare but we have rice in the menu every day.



The bottom line is that if you expect the food to be 5-star, you will surely be disappointed but if you expect it to be a decent meal/balanced diet, then you will never complain about it. If you want to eat other than mess timings, there is a canteen inside the Bhawan also (but don't expect any hygiene or sanitation). Canteen ‐



Anyhow, if you ever get bored of the mess food (which we all get from time to time) there is CCD, Sattviko Idea Café and Guest House restaurants inside the campus only and a bunch of places to eat outside the campus, including Domino’s and Pizza Hut.



So, food here is not at all a problem (although if you are a foodie and like to try new food every once in a while, get ready to be disappointed (especially if you are from Delhi), as the variety and choice of food is very limited).


Clothes - There is not much to talk about clothes. The only point worth sharing is that decent laundry service has been provided inside the Bhawan only, free of cost. You just have to put your clothes in a bucket, provide the required amount of detergent, keep the bucket in the laundry room, make an entry in the register and then collect them afterwards. (Laundry room of Rajendra Bhawan is situated in B-block ground floor)



[UPDATE 2021: In each bhawan (hostel), there will also be a laundry guy who would knock at your door on fixed days of the week, asking for clothes, to wash & iron them. Just be WARNED that they are NOT official people from the campus/bhawan. They are washermen ( धोबी ) from outside the campus who have been given permission to provide their services in the hostel in exchange for around Rs. 150-200 per month. In my experience, you absolutely DON'T need to opt in for this. They will force you nonetheless. Personally, many of my clothes were damaged when I opted for this in the initial days. Rest is upto you.]


Transportation - As the campus is very huge (almost 365 acres) and motor vehicles are not allowed, commuting is very tedious and time consuming (especially when you are getting late for that early morning 8’o clock lecture). Hence for commuting inside the campus, e‐rickshaws/tuk‐tuks are available which will take you anywhere inside the campus for a fixed minimal charge of ₹10. Otherwise, you can also use a bicycle (which indeed most of us end up doing).

[UPDATE 2021: Buying a bicycle wouldn't be a wise option in my opinion. Either it will be stolen in a few months or it will just sit and rot in the parking. I would suggest settle in for a few days without one. Then see if you really need one.]


Internet - Another “need” which you will realize after coming to the college (if you haven’t realized it till now) is to have an electronic device with you, be it a smartphone or a laptop, through which you can access the internet, because virtually, almost every piece of information here is transmitted in the form of electronic media only, be it assignments, notices or study material, everything is shared through LAN services provided inside the campus or through platforms like Google, WhatsApp, Facebook, etc.
Talking about LAN, the internet speeds here at IITR are just amazing and no restrictions have been placed whatsoever. You practically get 50+mbps up/down at minimum (which is the only thing which made me feel like home! :P). Wi-Fi is also provided all over the campus but it is unstable. Hence, internet connectivity is not at all an issue in IITR. Here is the screenshot of the speed test ‐



[UPDATE 2021: Some changes took place (Cisco happened :P) and the superfast LAN at IITR was just destroyed. Don't know the current situation, but still, I think it would be good enough.]


Hence, I would like to end this blog by saying that you are going to miss your “Home sweet Home”, but once you get used to things, IITRoorkee is a good place to live in.



[UPDATE 2021: I think hostel life is necessary as it helps us to get out of our comfort zones and when we are away from all the luxuries (you will understand later why I call them luxuries) we have at home, we graduate to the next level of maturity, which isn't always possible at home. Moreover, hostel life with friends has its own perks, and this is one thing that you are definitely going to miss after you pass out from college.]
